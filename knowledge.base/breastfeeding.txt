Poor latch can cause nipple pain and discomfort.

Engorgement happens when milk builds up and can be relieved with frequent feeding.

Low milk supply can improve with frequent feeding, hydration, and proper latch technique.

Postpartum stress is common and emotional support is important.
Breastfeeding should be comfortable and frequent in the early weeks.
Newborns typically feed 8–12 times per day.

Signs of good feeding include:
- audible swallowing
- relaxed baby after feeding
- regular wet diapers

If feeding is painful, it often indicates latch issues rather than normal soreness.
